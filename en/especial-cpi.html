everything you need to know about the parliamentary investigative committee's report on the pandemic

nexo jornal — 2021

How to transform a document of almost 1300 pages — of great public interest — into intelligible and navigable content?

The challenge was to turn the covid parliamentary investigative comittee's report into a special article, highlighting the most important points: the crimes reported, the targets of indictment and the possibilities of the report. And, furthermore, do not leave aside the political, social and health context investigated by the commission.

The solution was to dismember and reorganize the most important information. Present them in a layout with fewer visuals such as photos and illustrations, and more textual and interactive resources.

navigation

To make the content readily accessible, a sticky menu with anchors was developed for each section of the report — the reader does not have to scroll all the way down to the final content.

In addition to navigation, the menu also has the function of locating the reader: it detects and shows in which section of the special one is navigating.

counter

The 'Brazil Pandemic Timeline' section is long and detailed — it goes through all the key days since the beginning of the pandemic and its events. Its focus is to be reference material rather than fluid reading.

To give context to each of these days, a counter was developed alongside the text, which shows, on that day, how many deaths from covid had already been recorded in the country.

Its impact is to keep track of the number of victims increasing day by day, but also to see the number increase faster and faster on a faster scroll.

content boxes

To organize content with several topics, an organization by boxes was chosen, instead of the traditional plain text.

Each section has a set of buttons, which can be opened to reveal a box with the contents. For each section, the box needs a different design to accommodate different types of content.

The boxes also allow an overview of each section — without opening the items, it is possible to see the large amount of crimes investigated by the CPI, for example.

This project was developed using Adobe Xd and Visual Studio Code. The layout was designed together and under the direction of Rafaela Ranzani
