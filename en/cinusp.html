cinusp paulo emílio — university cinema

cinusp paulo emílio — 2015-2016

CINUSP is a free cinema at the University of São Paulo with screening rooms in the main campus and in the center of São Paulo, whose programming is organized through thematic exhibitions.

As a Visual Programming intern, I was the only designer on the team and was responsible for the visual identity of each show, for the production of graphic and digital materials for publicity. Printed materials — posters and booklets — are the main means of promoting cinema and communicating with its audience.

The main challenge was to include all the relevant information about the exhibition — name, image, program schedule and information from the film's technical sheets — in the same material, without making it less attractive. In some exhibitions, there was a need to include information such as the name of the director, main actress and even an indicative rating next to each film on the poster.

booklet

Publicity for the show also includes an A6-size booklet with 24 pages, which works as a small catalog of the show — it includes text by the curators, expanded grid, photos and fact sheets of all the films.

cinusp collection

In addition, CINUSP publishes a collection of books on cinema. The books are distributed free of charge to the public and to libraries throughout Brazil.

I was responsible for the layout and cover of two books in the collection. The first, "The cinema and the body", is the result of a project in partnership with teachers of the Physical Education school, which holds an annual exhibition. The second "Cinegrid: cinematic futures", is the product of a meeting of professionals and scholars of audiovisual technology, and condenses research and discoveries in the area.

This project was developed using Adobe Illustrator and Adobe Photoshop, under the direction of Thiago Afonso de André and Patrícia Moran
